Reopen Your Case

Just because a judge signed it doesn’t mean it’s over. If your case ended unfairly — if your attorney dropped the ball, if new evidence came to light, or if fraud was involved — you may be able to reopen it and fight again.

⚖️ Legal Tools to Reopen a Case

  • Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Judgment: Used when there was fraud, lack of notice, or serious error
  • Motion for New Trial: If your lawyer failed or new info would have changed the result
  • Appeal: If filed in time, you can challenge the outcome to a higher court

📝 What to Say in Your Motion

Include your name, case number, and exactly what was wrong — for example:

“My attorney failed to present critical evidence. I only discovered after judgment that [evidence or violation]. I request this case be reopened in the interest of justice.”
