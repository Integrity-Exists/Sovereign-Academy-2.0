Administrative Remedy 101

If you’ve been wronged by a government agency — like CPS, DSS, or even a jail — you have the right to demand an internal review or correction before filing a lawsuit. This is called the Administrative Remedy Process, and it creates a legal paper trail that proves you tried to resolve things first.

🛠️ Why Use It?

  • Shows the court you tried to resolve things before suing
  • Triggers a deadline for them to respond (and puts them on the record)
  • Can lead to internal corrections, case dismissals, or damages

📋 How It Works

  1. Write a clear grievance/notice letter explaining what happened and what right was violated
  2. Send it to the agency head or ombudsman, and keep proof of mailing
  3. Give them 10–30 days to respond (based on your state or agency policy)
  4. If they ignore it or deny without action, you can use that as evidence in court
