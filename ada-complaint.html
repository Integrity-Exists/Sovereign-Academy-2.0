Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Rights

If you have a disability, you have civil rights under federal law. The ADA protects access to employment, education, public services, and housing — nationwide.

Your Rights Under the ADA

  • 🚫 No discrimination in hiring, firing, or promotion
  • ♿ Access to public spaces and buildings is mandatory
  • 📝 Reasonable accommodations in work and school must be provided
  • ⚖️ You can sue under federal law if your rights are violated

Federal Enforcement

The ADA is enforced by:

  • U.S. Department of Justice (ADA Division)
  • EEOC for employment complaints
  • HUD for housing discrimination

Free Help & Legal Aid

  • ADA National Network Regional Centers
  • Disability Rights Organizations by State
  • Local legal aid offices (search: "[Your State] legal aid disability ADA")

How to File an ADA Complaint

  1. Gather documentation (dates, names, photos, etc.)
  2. File online via the DOJ Civil Rights Portal
  3. Or call the ADA Information Line: 1-800-514-0301
