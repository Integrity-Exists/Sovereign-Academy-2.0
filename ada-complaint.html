How to File an ADA Complaint

If you've been denied access, mistreated, or discriminated against because of a disability — you have the right to file an ADA complaint. This guide walks you through it step by step, with templates coming soon.

🛑 What Counts as a Violation?

  • DSS failing to accommodate a disability during interviews or court
  • Courtroom failing to provide a hearing aid, interpreter, or break schedule
  • Public buildings that are not accessible
  • Being punished or denied services for disability-related behavior

📋 Step-by-Step Process

  1. Document what happened – dates, names, and what was said or done
  2. Try to resolve it first with the agency (send a written request for accommodation)
  3. If ignored or denied, file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ)
  4. You can also file with your state’s ADA Coordinator or Human Rights Commission
  5. Keep copies of everything!
