🚨 What to Do If You're Arrested

Being arrested is terrifying. Even when you know your rights, it’s hard to think straight. That confusion? It’s by design.

This page is built to anchor you in the chaos. If you’re scared, overwhelmed, or just want to be ready — this is for you.

🛑 First Rule: SHUT UP

  • Say: "I invoke my right to remain silent."
  • Then say: "I want an attorney present before any questioning."
  • Then: Say NOTHING ELSE.

Talking your way out will not work. Cops are trained to get you talking. Anything you say WILL be used — twisted, clipped, taken out of context.

🤐 What NOT to Do

  • ❌ Don’t argue, resist, or try to explain
  • ❌ Don’t consent to searches ("I do not consent to a search")
  • ❌ Don’t lie — just stay silent

📜 Miranda Warnings

Police are only required to read your rights **if they question you after arrest.** If they skip it, it doesn’t mean your arrest is invalid — but it can impact admissibility in court. Ask Sage for details if this happened to you.

🧠 Ground Yourself

  • Breathe slow — fear makes you forget your training
  • Look for cameras — they may protect you
  • Memorize the officer’s badge number & name if possible

💬 Sample Phrase to Memorize:

"Officer, I do not consent to any searches, and I am exercising my right to remain silent. I will not answer questions without my attorney present."

📤 After the Arrest

  • 📞 Call someone you trust ASAP — give location, charges, and name
  • 🧾 Request a copy of the arrest report as soon as released
  • 🧑‍⚖️ If assigned a public defender, ask for a copy of discovery and case notes
  • 📚 Ask Sage or use Sovereign Academy to start prepping your defense

🧠 Final Thought

You may be judged for knowing your rights. That’s okay. Stay calm. Stay firm. Never forget: **You are not powerless.** You are just up against a system that benefits when you panic.
