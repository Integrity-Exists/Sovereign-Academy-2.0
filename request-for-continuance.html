Requesting a Court Continuance

Need more time before your court date? You can file a Request for Continuance (also called a Motion to Reschedule) to ask the judge to move the hearing to a later date. This is common and legal — especially for medical reasons, new evidence, or needing time to prepare.

📋 When You Can Ask

  • You just received notice and need more prep time
  • You have a schedule conflict (with proof)
  • You’re waiting on documents or records
  • You were recently hospitalized or have a disability

📝 How to Request It

  1. Write a short letter or motion titled “Request for Continuance”
  2. Include your name, case number, and scheduled court date
  3. State your reason and request a specific delay (example: 30 days)
  4. Attach any documents that support your reason (hospital paperwork, appointment slips, etc.)
  5. File with the Clerk and send a copy to the other party (if required)
