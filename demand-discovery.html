Demand Discovery & File a Motion to Compel

If the other side — especially DSS or CPS — refuses to hand over evidence, you don’t beg. You file a Demand for Discovery and, if they ignore it, you follow up with a Motion to Compel. No more games.

📦 What You Can Demand

  • Caseworker notes and internal memos
  • Photos, videos, recordings
  • Police reports and interview summaries
  • Names of witnesses or accusers
  • ICPC documents or foster care contracts

📝 How to Demand It

  1. Write a Demand for Discovery letter with your name, case number, and list of what you need
  2. Send it via certified mail or file it with the Clerk and serve the agency
  3. Wait 10–14 days for a response
  4. If they ignore it — file a Motion to Compel with proof of your request

🔥 Motion to Compel

The Motion to Compel tells the court the other party is stonewalling — and asks the judge to issue an order forcing them to turn it over. You can also ask for sanctions (punishment) if they refuse again.
