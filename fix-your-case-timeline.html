Court cases can feel like a maze ‚Äî especially if you're alone, overwhelmed, or under attack. This timeline gives you a step-by-step plan so you know what to do, when, and why. Whether you're dealing with CPS, criminal charges, or a civil dispute ‚Äî follow this as your battlefield map.

‚è∞ The General Timeline

  1. Get Your Paperwork: Read the court summons or DSS notice. Note dates & deadlines.
  2. File an Answer: If you're served, respond in writing (called an "Answer" or "Objection") within 21 days (most states).
  3. Request Discovery: Demand evidence right away (case notes, reports, interviews).
  4. Motion to Compel (if needed): If they ignore discovery, file this next.
  5. File Motions: Motion to Dismiss, Continuance, Suppress Evidence, etc. (as needed)
  6. Challenge Players: Motion to Remove GAL, Recuse Judge, Bar Complaints
  7. Request Hearing: If something urgent (custody, removal, etc.), ask for hearing date
  8. Prepare Evidence: Organize photos, texts, letters, statements
  9. Pretrial Filings: Submit witness list, exhibit list, trial brief (if required)
  10. Trial / Final Hearing: Bring all docs, call witnesses, ask questions
  11. Appeal (if needed): File Notice of Appeal within 10‚Äì30 days (varies by court)
