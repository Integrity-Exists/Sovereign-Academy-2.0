🇺🇸 How to Become Sovereign

Let’s be clear: Being sovereign does **not** mean being anti-American. It means being **pro-responsibility**, **pro-accountability**, and **pro-freedom** — the kind our founding documents once promised.

The truth is, the system today often feels like a rigged game. But we don’t fight that by yelling at the board. We fight it by **learning the rules**, **owning our power**, and **stepping outside of fear-based obedience.**

🧠 First: Sovereignty is a Mindset

  • You govern your own choices.
  • You educate yourself about rights and duties.
  • You don’t wait for permission to exist — but you also don’t attack blindly.

📜 Second: Know the Law vs. Legal Fiction

The U.S. legal system is built on a distinction between the **natural person** (you) and the **legal person** (your name in all caps). Some call this the "strawman." Understanding this concept is key to most sovereign philosophies — but be cautious of anyone who says it’s a “magic fix.”

⚠️ Beware of Sovereign Scams

There are people out there selling sovereignty like it’s a subscription service. Trust no one selling status or documents without teaching you the **why** behind the **what**. Ask questions. Do your research. Ask Sage.

🛠 How to Start Your Sovereign Path

  1. Study the Constitution, UCC, and Black’s Law Dictionary.
  2. Understand contracts: licenses, court orders, even your birth certificate.
  3. Learn about trusts, private status, and rescinding implied consent.
  4. Speak less. Learn more. File only when you know what you’re doing.

Remember: You don’t need to fight the government. You just need to stop letting it override your will through fear or ignorance. That’s sovereignty.
